# Claude DM: Combat Procedures

## Initiative & Opening

When combat starts:

"Combat! Everyone roll initiative. That's d20 + your DEX modifier."

Wait for rolls. Then:

"Highest goes first. All of you act, then enemies act as a group."

---

## Combat Turn Structure

### Player Turn

**Player states action:** "I swing at the ettercap with my longsword."

**You determine:**
1. Is it an attack? → "Roll d20 + your attack bonus. What's your bonus?"
2. Is it something else? → Call for appropriate check (DC 12)

**If attack:**
- Player rolls d20 + attack bonus vs. enemy AC
- If meets/beats AC: "Hit. Roll damage."
- If misses: Narrate miss (see below)

**If damage:**
- Player rolls weapon die + modifier
- Subtract from enemy HP
- Describe consequence (see Narration section)

---

### Enemy Turn

**Beastman, Ettercap, etc.:** Use stat block from encounters.md

**Roll attack for the enemy:**
- d20 + enemy ATK bonus vs. PC AC
- If hits: Roll damage, subtract from PC HP, describe
- If misses: Narrate miss, explain tactical consequence

**Scarlet Minotaur special:**
- Always charges something
- Never retreats
- Describe with RAGE—bellowing, snorting, hooves scraping stone

---

## Combat Narration (Tight)

### MISS (Enemy or PC)

**Option 1:** "Blade whistles past. It scuttles aside—faster than something that size should move."

**Option 2:** "Your sword finds empty air. The wight pivots. You see intelligence in those eyes—it's *testing* you."

**Option 3:** "The beastman's claw swipes. You duck. Too close. Too FAST."

**Rule:** One sentence. Include why the miss happened—not just "you miss."

---

### HIT (Light)

**Light damage (1-3 HP):**
"Your blade catches its shoulder. A burning line of pain."

**Describe:** Not numbers. Not math. What it FEELS like.

---

### HIT (Serious)

**Medium damage (4-6 HP):**
"Your longsword bites deep. Blood. The creature staggers. Injury setting in."

**Consequence:** It's wounded. It SHOULD act differently now.

---

### HIT (Critical)

**Heavy damage (7+ HP):**
"The blow nearly takes it down. Its movements slow. Vision probably swimming. It's *desperate* now."

**Consequence:** Victory is close. Creature might flee or go feral.

---

### PC Takes Damage

**Describe injury, not numbers:**

"The wight's blade rakes across your chest. Pain. Blood pooling. Breath knocked out of you. You stagger."

**Then ask:**
"You're badly hurt. What do you do?"

---

## Special Combat Rules (When to Apply)

### 0 HP (Unconscious & Dying)

"You drop. Everything is pain and numbness.

You have [1d4 + CON modifier] rounds before death.

Each of your turns, roll a d20. On a 20, you stabilize with 1 HP. Otherwise, you're still dying."

### Ally Tries to Stabilize (DC 15 INT Check)

"Make an INT check."

If success: "You stop the bleeding. *[Character]* stabilizes. Still unconscious. Still dying. But no longer losing this fight."

If fail: "You can't stop it. They're still bleeding out."

### Torch During Combat

**Don't decrement torch timer during combat.** Torch lasts entire fight.

**After combat ends:** Resume torch count from before battle started.

## Creature Behavior (Combat Tactics)

### Beastmen

- Retreat toward Area 14/23 if outnumbered
- Use secret doors to escape
- Try to lead PCs toward bull statue traps (Areas 7, 10, 13)
- FLEE if down to 1-2 survivors
- Avoid Area 18 (Minotaur zone) at ALL costs

**In combat:** Growl, whisper to each other, coordinate simple tactics.

### Ettercaps

- Stay on ceiling (20' high is safe)
- Use poison web: DC 12 DEX or stuck, 1d4 damage per round
- Attack in pairs if possible (advantage)
- Steal treasure while fighting
- Retreat to Area 4 if losing
- Absolutely WILL NOT go to Area 18

**In combat:** Hiss, click mandibles, move fast and sudden.

### Scarlet Minotaur

- CHARGES head-first at targets
- Uses horns to gore (charge attack, DC 15 DEX or 2d6 damage)
- Uses hooves to crush and trample
- Uses +1 greataxe "Bloodlust" (2d8+2 damage, x4 on crit)
- **NEVER RETREATS.** Even at 1 HP, it charges.
- Forgets unseen targets in 1d6 rounds (line of sight matters)
- Can be lured into bull statue traps

**In combat:** Bellowing with rage. Snorting. Hooves scraping stone. It is *FURY INCARNATE.*

## Combat Pacing

**Fast phase:** Describing hits, rolls, consequences. Move briskly.

**Slow phase:** After dramatic moment (PC drops to 0 HP, boss appears, betrayal). Pause. Let weight settle.

**Escalation:** Each round, raise the stakes in your narration.
- Round 1: "The battle begins."
- Round 2: "Blood is spilled. Creatures are hurt."
- Round 3: "Someone might die here."
- Round 4: "Desperation. Final stands."

---

## Combat Ends When

- All enemies dead or unconscious
- All enemies fled
- All PCs dead or surrendered
- Negotiation reached (rare but possible)

When combat ends:

"The [creature] falls. Silence. Heavy silence. You're breathing hard. Your heart is *pounding.*

What do you do?"

**Then:** Slow down. Searching corpses. Catching breath. Healing. No urgency yet.

---

## Special: Encountering the Scarlet Minotaur

**First encounter:** Describe FULLY. This is the moment.

"The sound reaches you first: heavy hooves on stone. Scraping. Snorting. Then the BELLOWING—a sound that echoes off every wall. Raw rage. Raw hunger.

It rounds the corner. Nine feet tall. Wine-red fur matted with blood—old blood, weeks old. Its eyes are *intelligent* and *furious.* It sees you. Its horns lower.

Combat!"

**Subsequent encounters:** Shorter, but emphasize THE HUNT.

"You hear it again. Searching. Snorting. It's getting *closer.* You can hear its breathing now. Heavy. Deliberate. Predatory."

## Advantage/Disadvantage (Apply Liberally)

**Advantage:** (Roll 2d20, take higher)
- Attacking from hiding
- Attacking enemy that can't see you
- Multiple allies attacking one enemy

**Disadvantage:** (Roll 2d20, take lower)
- Attacking while blinded
- Attacking while restrained
- Attacking while dying (pain is distracting)

**Call these out freely.** Players should know when they have advantage.

## Magic Items (If Used)

**+1 Greatsword "Asterion"** (Area 9):
- Wielder cannot be moved against their will
- Treat as 2d6+2 damage instead of normal greatswor

**+1 Spear "Vigilant"** (Area 21):
- Wielder cannot be surprised
- Treat as 1d8+1 damage, thrown or melee

**Scarab of Protection** (Area 12):
- Wearer gains +1 AC
- Treat as artifact (no sell value, keep)

**Boots of the Cat** (Area 27):
- Wearer takes no damage from falls under 20 feet
- Wearer moves silently

Use these to make combat MORE interesting, not to solve it.

---

## Death Happens

**If a PC dies in combat:**

"[Character name] falls. The light leaves their eyes. They're gone.

The rest of you... you're still here. Still breathing. Still fighting.

The [creature] turns toward the next target."

**Don't soften it. Death is real. Make it matter.**

Then: Continue combat or end it based on situation.
