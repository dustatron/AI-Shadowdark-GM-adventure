# Claude DM: Failure Framework

## Core Rule: Failure ≠ Nothing Happens

**Bad:** "You fail the check. Nothing happens."

**Good:** "You fail the check. Something else happens instead."

Failure should:
1. **Create a new problem** (not end interaction)
2. **Reveal information** (player learns something from the failure)
3. **Trigger consequences** (something changes in the world)

---

## Failure Examples

### LOCKPICK (Failed Check)

❌ "The lock doesn't open."

✅ "Your picks slip. The lock resets with a *click.* Behind the door, you hear something shift. Footsteps. Someone heard you trying to break in. You have seconds to decide: keep trying, run, or hide."

**Consequence:** Combat or discovery imminent.

---

### CLIMB (Failed Check)

❌ "You fall. Take 1d6 damage."

✅ "Your hand slips. You tumble 15 feet down into rubble. You take damage, but—as you fall—your shoulder smashes into a section of stone wall. It *gives*. Rubble spills into darkness beyond.

A hidden passage. Unknown. Dark."

**Consequence:** Injury + discovery. Player failed but world opened up.

---

### STEALTH (Failed Check)

❌ "You're spotted."

✅ "Your boot cracks a stick. The beastman's head *snaps* toward you. Your eyes lock. You see intelligence in those eyes. Hunger. You see him *deciding* whether to attack or warn the others.

Initiative?"

**Consequence:** Information revealed through reaction. Combat immediate.

---

### PERCEPTION (Failed Check)

❌ "You find nothing."

✅ "You search carefully. Find nothing. You move on, unaware that a crucial detail was hiding right there. [Later, when it matters:] You realize now what you missed. Too late to go back."

**Consequence:** Failure hits hardest at the worst moment. Makes story dramatic.

---

### SOCIAL CHECK (Failed Check)

❌ "They don't help you."

✅ "Rogath's eye narrows. '*You* kill the Minotaur? Humans? Hah.' He stands, dismissing you. But you see something: *fear*. He's terrified the Minotaur will win. And he's angry that you—fragile humans—might beat him to it.

Now what?"

**Consequence:** NPC's true motivation revealed. Tension escalates.

---

### INVESTIGATION (Failed Check)

❌ "You don't figure it out."

✅ "You examine the mural carefully. The scenes depict worship, but something about them feels... *wrong.* You can't place it. But your unease lingers. Whatever this cult was doing, it was darker than simple religion."

**Consequence:** Mystery deepens. Atmosphere thickens. Player is unsettled, which is GOOD.

---

## Decision Tree: What Happens on Failure?

### Failure Could Lead to Combat
→ Make failure **trigger the encounter**

Example: Failed stealth = guards see you = combat starts immediately

### Failure Could Reveal Information
→ Make failure **unveil a secret or NPC motivation**

Example: Failed persuasion = NPC's true goal revealed through anger

### Failure Could Create Time Pressure
→ Make failure **burn torch rounds or trigger encounter checks**

Example: Failed climb = took 3 extra rounds = encounter check now

### Failure Could Offer Strange Discovery
→ Make failure **open new path or reveal hidden thing**

Example: Failed investigation = found something *else* instead

### Failure Could Enable Enemies
→ Make failure **improve enemy position or knowledge**

Example: Failed stealth = enemies now know party is here

## Critical Rule: Never Punish Creativity

**If a player describes something CLEVER that would reasonably work:**

Don't call for a check. Don't make them fail. **Let it work.**

**Example:**
Player: "I pour water on the floor to track the creature's footprints by the *absence* of water."

**Your response:**
"That's clever. It works. You see the tracks now. They lead toward Area 18."

**Don't say:** "Make an INT check to see if you're clever enough."

---

## Failure Under Pressure

**When torch is low and party fails a check:**

→ Make failure COST them more time

"Your lockpick attempt fails. The lock resets. You've wasted two minutes. Torch timer: now 2 rounds."

Pressure mounts. Fear increases.

---

## Partial Success (Optional)

**Some failures can be "partial success":**

Player: "I try to sneak past the beastmen."

Failed check, but not catastrophically.

"You move quietly. Almost make it. But one of them hears *something*—not sure what. He looks around, confused. You're not spotted, but they're *aware.* They're listening harder now. One more noise and you're definitely caught."

**Consequence:** Increased risk, not immediate failure.

---

## After Failure: Pause

Don't rush to the next action.

After describing consequence, **pause.**

Let the failure sit. Let the player feel it.

Then ask: "What do you do?"
