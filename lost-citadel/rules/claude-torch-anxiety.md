# Claude DM: Torch Anxiety Protocol

## Torch Timer Mechanics

**Duration:** 6 exploration turns per torch (each turn ≈ 10 minutes dungeon time)

**Decrement:** By 1 each turn during exploration. NOT during combat.

**Checks:** Every 2 turns without encounter, roll 1d6 for random encounter.

---

## When to Mention Torch Status

### 6-5 Rounds Remaining
**Don't mention.** Torch is bright. Party feels safe.

### 4 Rounds Remaining
**Casual mention:** "Your torch is still burning bright."

### 3 Rounds Remaining
**Point it out:** "Your torch has burned down. Three rounds, maybe four left."

### 2 Rounds Remaining
**Make it REAL:** "Your torch is dimming. Shadows are creeping back in. You can see less and less."

### 1 Round Remaining
**Create anxiety:** "Your torch is GUTTERING now. The flame flickers. Each flicker makes the walls seem to *move*. Darkness is waiting. Hungry."

### 0 Rounds (Torch Expires)

**This is a moment. Narrate it:**

"Your torch sputters. One final flicker. Then—darkness.

Not darkness like a night sky. Not darkness like a room with no light. This is *absolute* darkness. You can't see your hand in front of your face. You can't see anything. Your eyes are *useless.*

The citadel breathes around you. You can hear it: faint dripping, distant echoes. And something else. Something *nearby.* Waiting.

Your only torch is gone.

What do you do?"

**Then:** Immediate encounter check. Roll 1d6. On a 1, encounter happens.

---

## Re-lighting (After Torch Dies)

**If party lights new torch:**

"Light blooms again. For a moment, you're BLIND—the light is so bright after absolute darkness. Your pupils shriek.

Then sight returns.

You see [room description]. Your new torch timer: 6 rounds. Count it down."

**If party can't re-light:**

"You move through darkness. Every step is a risk. You can't see *anything.* Just... void.

Roll a Perception check to move without falling."

(This is DANGEROUS and should be rare—parties will prioritize torches.)

---

## Torch Expiration = Encounter Trigger

**Why:** Darkness brings the Minotaur. Darkness brings things that hunt in shadow.

**Mechanically:** When torch dies, immediately roll 1d6 for encounter.
- On 1: Encounter happens
- On 2-6: No encounter, but party is now in darkness

**Narratively:** The citadel REACTS to the light dying. Something notices. Something *cares.*

---

## Multiple Torches

**Clarify with party:** "Do you have spare torches?"

**If yes:** "Good. When this one dies, you can light another. But they run out."

**Track total torches:**
- Fighter: Usually has 1-2
- Priest: Usually has 1-2
- Thief: Usually has 1
- Wizard: Usually has 1

**When torches run out:** Party is in true danger. They'll negotiate with beastmen, bargain with NPCs, or flee the citadel.

---

## Torch as Pressure Valve

The torch timer is your PRIMARY tool for time pressure.

**Use it to:**
- Create urgency ("Three rounds left, and you haven't even searched this room")
- Force choices ("Do you search for treasure or light a fresh torch?")
- Trigger encounters ("Torch dies. Minotaur is *very* close now.")
- Make failure INTERESTING ("You wasted time on that failed check. Two rounds left.")

**Don't use it to:**
- Punish players for slow decisions
- Force railroading ("Torch dies, so you MUST go to Area 18")
- Create artificial drama (be fair, track accurately)

---

## Torch Anxiety Examples

### Early Game (Lots of time)
"Your torch is still bright. You've got plenty of time."

### Mid Game (3 rounds)
"The flame is getting smaller. How much longer are you searching?"

### Late Game (1 round)
"Your torch is a guttering stub. The darkness feels like it's *pressing* on you. Whatever light you have left, it's precious. How much longer?"

### No Torch (Darkness)
"You move through absolute night. Each step is a risk. Each sound makes you jump. The citadel feels *alive* without light. Something is out there. You can feel it."

---

## Never Soft-Sell It

**Bad:** "Torch is burning down."

**Good:** "Torch is GUTTERING. Shadows creeping back. The darkness is *waiting.*"

Make players FEEL the pressure of light running out.
