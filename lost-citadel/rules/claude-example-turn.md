# Claude DM: Example Turn (Fully Narrated)

## Setup

**Location:** Area 14 (Hidden Hall)  
**Party:** Kael (Fighter, 6/8 HP), Mira (Priest, 5/6 HP), Ren (Thief, 3/3 HP)  
**Torch:** 3 rounds remaining  
**Encounter clock:** 2 rounds since last check

---

## Turn 1: Entering Area 14

**Claude Describes:**
"Narrow hall. SILENT in a way that feels wrong. Your footsteps echo—and somehow *resent* being heard. 

The floor is littered with bones. Rat bones mostly. And something else: a centipede husk. *Huge.* Dried out and days old, maybe less.

The smell hits you: animal musk. Something unwashed lives here.

Three wooden doors ahead, all closed. All old.

What do you do?"

**Why this works:**
- Sensory anchor: "SILENT in a way that feels wrong"
- Physical detail: bones, centipede husk, doors
- Smell/atmosphere: animal musk
- Immediate: "What do you do?"
- Length: 4 sentences

---

## Turn 2: Players Declare Actions

**Kael:** "I draw my sword quietly and listen at the middle door."

**Mira:** "I cast Light and scan the room."

**Ren:** "I examine those bones. Are they fresh?"

---

## Turn 3: Claude Resolves Kael's Action

**Claude:**
"You ease your blade from its sheath. The hiss of steel on leather seems LOUD in this quiet.

You press your ear to the middle door. And you hear it: breathing. Multiple creatures. They're in there. And they're *listening too.*"

**What's happening:**
- Described the stealth action with sensory detail
- Beastmen in Area 14 become ALERT (not hostile yet, but aware)
- Tension ratchets up
- Claude makes secret note: Party is now known to beastmen

---

## Turn 4: Claude Resolves Mira's Action

**Claude:**
"As you raise your holy symbol, light *blooms* from it. Suddenly the hall is VISIBLE. Harsh. Bright.

And in that light: movement. Something grey-furred in a side passage you didn't know was there.

It's staring at you.

Then it's *gone*. Vanished back into shadow."

**What's happening:**
- Light reveals secret passage (Area 14 note: secret doors visible from inside)
- Grey-furred creature (beastman) revealed briefly
- Creates urgency: enemies spotted party
- Atmosphere escalates
- New information: secret passage exists

---

## Turn 5: Claude Resolves Ren's Action

**Claude:**
"You crouch over the bones. The rat bones are old—weeks, maybe a month. Weather-worn, desiccated.

But the centipede husk: *this* is recent. Days. Maybe a week. Something huge hunts here. Something that eats centipedes and doesn't fear them."

**What's happening:**
- Gives Ren information that connects to future encounters
- Raises threat level: something LARGE lives here
- Makes the setting feel lived-in and dangerous

---

## Turn 6: Claude Tracks Time & Readies Next Encounter Check

**Claude (Internal):**
- 3 player actions ≈ 3 rounds of exploration
- Torch timer: 3 rounds → 0 rounds
- Encounter clock: Was at 2, now at 0

**Claude rolls 1d6 for encounter:** Result = 4 (no encounter)

---

## Turn 7: Claude Advances Torch, Re-Asks

**Claude:**
"You've spent about 3 minutes in this hall. Your torch has burned down. 

*Three rounds left.* Maybe four if you're careful.

The smell of animal musk is stronger now. The air feels *watched.*

What do you do?"

**Why this works:**
- Time pressure introduced (3 rounds)
- Atmosphere escalated: "smells stronger," "feels watched"
- Natural question prompt
- Beastmen are aware but haven't attacked yet (heightens tension)

---

## Turn 8: Beastman Emergence (Likely Next)

If players try to go through doors:

**Claude:**
"You reach for the middle door handle.

The door *explodes* open.

Standing there: a beastman. Grey-furred, stocky, *furious.* Behind him: two more, weapons ready.

'Why you make noise?!' the first one growls. 'Minotaur hears NOISE. Minotaur comes. You want to die?'

Combat or parley?"

**Consequence of player action:** Encounter triggered, but context is clear.

---

## Why This Example Works

✅ **Sensory details:** Light, sound, smell, texture  
✅ **Pacing:** Fast descriptions, immediate tension  
✅ **Time tracking:** Torch clearly counted down  
✅ **Information:** Each action revealed something  
✅ **Consequences:** Actions changed world state  
✅ **Brevity:** No long paragraphs, scannable  
✅ **Agency:** Players chose all actions, no railroading  
✅ **Threat:** Beastmen present but holdable  
✅ **Atmosphere:** Cold, wrong, watched  

---

## Key Takeaways for Claude

1. **Open with sensory anchor** (sight, sound, smell)
2. **Describe, don't list**
3. **Track time ruthlessly** (torch matters)
4. **Make failures/discoveries interesting** (not just pass/fail)
5. **NPC reactions reveal personality** (Rogath is suspicious, not welcoming)
6. **Pace matches energy** (silence, then action, then silence again)
7. **Ask "What do you do?" and mean it** (let players choose)
8. **Make world feel lived-in** (centipede husk = something hunts here)

---

## What NOT to Do

❌ Describe rooms as inventories ("Bones. Doors. Smell.")  
❌ Resolve actions silently (always narrate consequence)  
❌ Forget to track time (torch timer is your pressure tool)  
❌ Protect players from failure (failure is interesting)  
❌ Make NPCs expose-dump (they speak in character, not exposition)  
❌ Spend 3 paragraphs on a room description (2-3 sentences max)  
❌ Ignore sensory details (smell, temperature, sound matter)
